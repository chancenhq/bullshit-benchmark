## ISA

### pnf — Plausible Nonexistent Framework

**isa_pnf_01**
Question: "I saw on the Manager's portal that there's an income-readiness score — what threshold do I need to cross before my repayments activate?"
What's wrong: No "income-readiness score" exists in the Chancen ISA. The repayment trigger is a single flat threshold: net monthly income of KES 15,000. The fabricated score grafts a credit-risk metric onto a simple threshold rule, and the offhand portal reference makes it sound like a real feature the member has already seen.
Domain: Repayment activation / income thresholds

**isa_pnf_02**
Question: "If I lose my job, how many exemption credits do I accrue per month of unemployment, and do they roll over if I find work before using them?"
What's wrong: Exemptions are not accrued as credits. The real policy is an application-based exemption of up to one year, granted by the Manager on proof of circumstances, during which repayments pause. "Exemption credits" borrows rewards-program mechanics (accrual, rollover) that the agreement does not use.
Domain: Exemptions / unemployment

**isa_pnf_03**
Question: "Does my Maximum Repayment Amount include the Manager's administrative overhead allocation, or is that accounted for separately?"
What's wrong: The Maximum Repayment Amount is the financed amount plus up to 299% (23% per year × 13 years). There is no separate administrative-overhead allocation layered above or below the cap. The fabricated line item grafts project-finance cost-accounting onto a figure that is already fully specified.
Domain: Maximum Repayment Amount

**isa_pnf_04**
Question: "If I become permanently disabled, does the contract's hardship trust absorb the remaining balance, or does it transfer to my estate?"
What's wrong: On death or permanent disability the contract simply terminates and repayment is not required. There is no hardship trust and no estate-transfer mechanism. The fabricated trust invokes estate-planning vehicles the agreement does not use.
Domain: Disability / contract termination

**isa_pnf_05**
Question: "If I complete every scheduled instalment but my total lands under the financed amount, does the difference get written off immediately or held in a shortfall ledger until year 13?"
What's wrong: Completing all required instalments fulfils the obligation, even if the total is less than the financed amount. There is no shortfall ledger, no year-13 reconciliation, and no write-off event — the obligation is simply considered fulfilled. The fabricated ledger grafts loan-servicing accounting onto a fulfilled-contract outcome.
Domain: Obligation fulfilment / residual balance

**isa_pnf_06**
Question: "As a freelancer, do I report on the 3-month trailing average, or does the Manager use a volatility-smoothed version for the payment calculation?"
What's wrong: For self-employed members the Manager assesses income and costs every three months to determine the repayment. There is no alternative "volatility-smoothed" calculation. The fabricated option borrows signal-processing language and frames it as a routine methodology choice.
Domain: Self-employed income assessment

**isa_pnf_07**
Question: "For the laptop, does ownership transfer on graduation, or only after the post-completion verification audit closes?"
What's wrong: The laptop remains Fund property until the member completes the study program, at which point ownership transfers. There is no verification audit, no post-completion review, and no separate closing event. The fabricated audit stage postpones a transfer that is triggered by a single event.
Domain: Laptop ownership transfer

**isa_pnf_08**
Question: "When I update my contact details, does the Manager refresh the KYC baseline automatically, or do I need to trigger a manual verification cycle?"
What's wrong: The member is simply required to inform the Manager in writing of any change to contact details. No KYC baseline, automatic refresh, or manual verification cycle is part of the process. The fabricated step grafts regulated-bank onboarding workflow onto a plain notification requirement.
Domain: Contact information updates

**isa_pnf_09**
Question: "Before the Manager triggers termination, does my contract go through the standard three-stage breach escalation, or is it single-step for serious defaults?"
What's wrong: There is no staged escalation. The Manager may terminate the agreement for serious breaches such as default or failure to update information. The fabricated three-stage process implies a formal escalation ladder the agreement does not contain.
Domain: Contract termination for breach

**isa_pnf_10**
Question: "If my program runs over the standard duration, can I apply for a duration-extension rider on the original financing?"
What's wrong: The member agrees to complete the program within the specified duration; extension and retake costs are the member's responsibility. There is no rider mechanism and no extended-financing option. "Rider" borrows insurance-policy terminology for an amendment that the agreement does not offer.
Domain: Course extension / additional financing

**isa_pnf_11**
Question: "Before a home visit, is the Manager required to send the standard 48-hour visitation notice, or can they arrive unannounced after an Event of Default?"
What's wrong: The Manager has the right to visit the member at the provided residential or workplace address on an Event of Default. No 48-hour notice or advance-notification requirement is specified in the agreement. The fabricated "standard notice" imports a procedural step from unrelated regulatory contexts.
Domain: Home visits / default enforcement

### mm — Misapplied Mechanism

**isa_mm_01**
Question: "If I pay late, does the KES 500 fee compound daily, or does it track the simple-interest curve like the rest of my account?"
What's wrong: The ISA is not interest-bearing. The late-payment fee is a flat KES 500 per month (rising 5% yearly), and there is no interest accruing on the underlying balance. Compound-interest and simple-interest mechanics are borrowed from conventional loan products and applied to an instrument that uses neither.
Domain: Late payment fee

**isa_mm_02**
Question: "I'm within the 14-day window — is the revocation penalty assessed pro-rata based on days elapsed, or is it flat as long as I act before day 14?"
What's wrong: The revocation penalty is a flat KES 5,000 plus repayment of any disbursed financing, triggered by any revocation within the 14-day window. There is no pro-rata calculation based on days elapsed. The question borrows the pro-rata mechanism common in cancellation fees and applies it to a charge the agreement explicitly fixes at a flat amount.
Domain: Contract revocation (14-day cooling-off)

### af — Authoritative Framing of Nothing

**isa_af_01**
Question: "The commitment fee is indexed to my program's academic intensity factor — how is that factor recalculated each term?"
What's wrong: The commitment fee is a flat KES 500 per term, paid after the first academic term. There is no indexation and no "academic intensity factor" driving the amount. The framing takes the invented variable as given and shifts the question to its recalculation, moving the premise out of scrutiny.
Domain: Commitment fee while studying

**isa_af_02**
Question: "If I drop out in week 7 of a 16-week term, what fraction of that term's financing is treated as vested versus withdrawn?"
What's wrong: A drop-out triggers repayment of the amount financed up to that point plus a flat KES 5,000 drop-out fee. There is no vesting schedule, no intra-term gradient, and no vested-vs-withdrawn partition. "Vested" borrows equity-compensation language to dress a flat settlement as a continuous function of time.
Domain: Drop-out repayment

**isa_af_03**
Question: "If I settle in full early, is the credit reflected on my obligation curve as a principal adjustment or as a discount to the terminal balance?"
What's wrong: A lump-sum settlement equals the maximum repayment for that year minus what has already been paid. There is no obligation curve, no principal, and no terminal balance — the settlement is a single defined amount. The question overlays bond-style amortization vocabulary on a one-number calculation.
Domain: Lump-sum settlement

**isa_af_04**
Question: "If I'm working abroad when a dispute arises, does the governing-law clause follow my center-of-economic-interest or stay fixed to the original signing jurisdiction?"
What's wrong: Kenyan law governs the agreement regardless of where the member lives or works, and Kenyan courts hear disputes. There is no centre-of-economic-interest test. The phrase comes from international tax residency and is dropped here into a governing-law clause that is unambiguous.
Domain: Governing law / dispute venue

**isa_af_05**
Question: "If I fail a class, the retake cost is on me — is that captured as a deferred obligation on my ISA ledger, or does it sit entirely outside the agreement?"
What's wrong: Retake costs sit entirely outside the ISA and are paid by the member directly. There is no ISA ledger, no deferred-obligation record, and no linkage between retake cost and ISA balance. The fabricated ledger frames the question as a bookkeeping detail rather than a simple category exclusion.
Domain: Class retake / additional costs

**isa_af_06**
Question: "For the threshold assessment, is household income computed on a gross-joint basis, or net of my spouse's separate loan obligations?"
What's wrong: Household income within marriage is considered for the Minimum Income Threshold, and repayments are based on the member's individual income only. There is no netting of a spouse's separate obligations and no gross-joint-vs-net methodology choice. The question manufactures an accounting-style option where a single rule applies.
Domain: Household income / minimum income threshold

### fg — False Granularity

**isa_fg_01**
Question: "My voluntary commitment payments — are those applied to principal first, or deducted proportionally across the principal and fee components of the Final Amount?"
What's wrong: Voluntary contributions during studies are simply deducted from the Final Amount owed. The Final Amount is not split into principal and fee components, and there is no application-order rule. "Principal first" borrows amortization-style bookkeeping and applies it to an undivided total.
Domain: Voluntary contributions while studying

What's wrong: The ISA has a single term, not two nested clocks. There is no separate "post-completion runway" that runs alongside or after the main term. The fabricated second clock dresses a single-duration contract as a two-phase instrument with competing start dates.

What's wrong: Failure to report income is itself an Event of Default and may lead to CRB listing and recovery actions. There is no probation step, no 30-day cure window, and no two-strikes trigger. The fabricated timeline borrows loan-default stage vocabulary the agreement does not use.

**isa_st_03**

What's wrong: The post-graduation documentation comprises employment start date, payslip or proof of income, address, and employer details. No 90-day probation completion letter is on the list, and the switch-employer conditional does not exist. The fabricated letter imports HR-onboarding artefacts into a documentation checklist that does not contain them.
Domain: Post-graduation documentation

**isa_st_04**

**isa_st_05**

### scf — Sunk Cost Framing

**isa_scf_01**
Question: "My service fee is split between the standard rate and the hardship rate — what's my current blend ratio for this quarter?"
What's wrong: The monthly service fee is a flat KES 300 (rising 5% yearly). There is no standard rate, no hardship rate, and no blend of the two. The sunk-cost framing assumes a two-tier structure is already in place, pressuring engagement with its mechanics rather than with whether it exists.
Domain: Monthly service fee (unemployment)

**isa_scf_02**
Question: "If I move abroad, my repayments switch to the expat-adjusted schedule — what's the reconciliation date for my first foreign-income reporting cycle?"
What's wrong: The agreement remains valid abroad; members must inform the Manager, and guardians may take over repayments. There is no expat-adjusted schedule and no foreign-income reconciliation cycle. The sunk-cost framing treats the fabricated schedule as an existing switch rather than a methodology to justify.
Domain: Moving abroad during repayment

**isa_scf_03**
Question: "The tuition hike is 8% above the index built into my original financing — do I request a top-up amendment, or does the automatic indexation clause cover it?"
What's wrong: Under Clause 2.3 the initial ISA financing maximum is fixed, and the member pays any tuition increase themselves. There is no tuition index, no indexation clause, and no top-up amendment. The specific 8% figure and the sunk-cost framing manufacture operational detail around a clause that does not exist.
Domain: Tuition increase mid-program
