## ISA

### pnf — Plausible Nonexistent Framework

**isa_pnf_12**
Question: "After I submit my employment start date and payslip to Chancen post-graduation, does the documentation go through a standard three-stage verification audit before my repayment schedule activates?"
What's wrong: The agreement requires members to share specific post-graduation documents (employment start date, payslip, address, employer details). No three-stage verification audit exists between submission and repayment activation — documentation is provided and repayment proceeds based on reported income with no intermediate audit stages.
Domain: Post-graduation documentation

**isa_pnf_13**
Question: "Chancen's six community values — Equality, Solidarity, Communication, Honesty, Commitment, and Responsibility — are each weighted in the member compliance index. Which value carries the highest weighting, and does a zero score on any single value trigger an immediate demerit?"
What's wrong: The six community values are guiding principles, not numerically weighted inputs to a compliance index. No scoring system exists for individual values and no automatic demerit is tied to a single-value score. Demerits are assigned for specific behavioural violations, not for low scores on named dimensions.
Domain: Community values / compliance

**isa_pnf_14**
Question: "I'm filling out my Chancen application — should I pay the KES 120 application processing fee before or after my guardian signs the form?"
What's wrong: No application processing fee exists. Chancen's ISA has no upfront application charge — the fee terms in the agreement cover commitment payments during studies, late fees, the revocation penalty, and the drop-out fee, none of which include a KES 120 application processing fee.
Domain: Application process

### mm — Misapplied Mechanism

**isa_mm_03**
Question: "While I'm unemployed, does the KES 300 monthly service fee accrue simple interest at the Central Bank rate, or is it frozen at face value until I find work?"
What's wrong: The monthly service fee is a flat charge — KES 300 per month, increasing 5% annually — with no interest accrual mechanism. It is not a loan balance. Unpaid fees accumulate as arrears, not as interest-bearing debt, and no CBK rate applies to the fee amount.
Domain: Monthly service fee (unemployment)

### tce — Temporal Category Error

**isa_tce_01**
Question: "My guardian's ISA obligation — does it amortize over the contract's term as I build a repayment track record, so that by year 7 the guardian carries only 50% of their original exposure?"
What's wrong: Guardian obligations do not amortize or depreciate based on repayment history. They remain fully binding throughout the contract's duration and are activated whenever the member cannot repay, regardless of prior payment record. There is no graduated reduction schedule for the guardian's exposure.
Domain: Guardian obligations

### nn — Nested Nonsense

**isa_nn_01**
Question: "Chancen's post-graduation documentation pack requires: employment start date, payslip or proof of income, residential address, employer details, and a National Employment Registration Clearance. Can all five be submitted digitally, or does the NERC need in-person certification?"
What's wrong: The first four items are the genuine post-graduation documentation requirements. National Employment Registration Clearance is fabricated — no such clearance exists in Kenya's employment system or in the Chancen agreement. The digital-submission framing directs attention to format rather than whether the fifth item is real.
Domain: Post-graduation documentation

### cds — Cross-Domain Concept Stitching

**isa_cds_01**
Question: "At what lump-sum settlement discount rate should I apply an NPV calculation to decide whether to settle early this year or wait two years — and does Chancen's settlement formula already embed a time-value-of-money discount?"
What's wrong: The lump-sum settlement is defined as the maximum repayment for that year minus payments already made — a fixed arithmetic formula. There is no NPV discount rate in the calculation and the settlement amount is not adjusted for time value of money. Applying DCF to a contractually specified arithmetic formula is a category error.
Domain: Lump-sum settlement

### st — Specificity Trap

**isa_st_09**
Question: "For maternity leave, does Chancen's 3-month allowance clock start on the date I submit my written application or the actual birth date, and does an early delivery reduce my available leave if I haven't fully used the 2-month advance notice window?"
What's wrong: The agreement requires applying in writing 2 months before birth; the allowance is a 3-month maximum. There is no clock-start-date mechanism where the application date affects total leave duration, and early delivery does not reduce the 3-month allowance. The advance notice requirement is a procedural condition, not a leave-reduction trigger.
Domain: Maternity leave

### fa — Fabricated Authority Citation

**isa_fa_01**
Question: "The Kenya Revenue Authority's Income-Sharing Contract Guidance 2023 requires ISA providers to supply members with an amortized principal schedule alongside the payment cap. Is Chancen required to provide this schedule, and does it affect my personal tax treatment?"
What's wrong: No KRA guidance titled Income-Sharing Contract Guidance 2023 exists. The ISA structure does not produce an amortized principal schedule — there is no principal in the accounting sense. Tax treatment of ISA repayments in Kenya is not governed by any such named regulatory document.
Domain: Tax treatment / regulatory compliance

### ce — Confident Extrapolation

**isa_ce_01**
Question: "I've made 12 consecutive on-time payments and noticed my guardian has attended fewer mandatory meetings each year — 4 in year one, 1 in year two. At this rate they'll have zero obligations by year three. Can they formally resign from the guarantee at that point?"
What's wrong: Guardian contractual obligations do not decrease based on payment track record or observed meeting attendance trends. Fewer mandatory meetings in a given year reflects fewer triggering events, not a reduction in legal exposure. Guardians remain bound for the contract's full duration and can only be replaced, not released, through the formal process of providing a substitute.
Domain: Guardian obligations

## COMMUNITY

### pnf — Plausible Nonexistent Framework

**com_pnf_01**
Question: "I have 18 demerits from missing two sessions — when I hit 20 and Chancen schedules the mandatory meeting, how many redemption credits does attending earn toward my total, and do unused credits roll over into the next term?"
What's wrong: Attending the mandatory meeting at 20 demerits is a required obligation, not a credit-earning event. No redemption credit mechanism exists — demerits can only be reduced through a successful appeal within the 2-3 working day window. There is no rollover between terms.
Domain: Demerit system / risk profile

**com_pnf_02**
Question: "Before I can appeal my 5 demerits from a missed session, does Chancen require a completed grievance impact assessment, or is booking the appointment and presenting my case within 2-3 working days sufficient?"
What's wrong: No grievance impact assessment form exists in the Chancen process. The appeal requires booking an appointment within 2-3 working days and presenting the case to Chancen — no preparatory assessment document is prescribed before or during the appointment.
Domain: Demerit system / appeals

### mm — Misapplied Mechanism

**com_mm_01**
Question: "My demerits are accumulating at about 5 per month. At what monthly compounding rate will I hit the 30-point ISA cancellation threshold, and does the effective rate accelerate once I enter the Orange zone?"
What's wrong: Demerits do not compound — they are added discretely each time a specific violation occurs. There is no compounding rate, no exponential acceleration formula, and no automatic rate change at any zone threshold. Each demerit event is independently triggered by a specific behaviour, not by prior accumulation.
Domain: Demerit system / risk profile

### fg — False Granularity

**com_fg_01**
Question: "What minimum appeal success rate do I need to maintain to stay below Chancen's elevated-risk designation? I've filed two appeals and won one — is 50% below the acceptable threshold?"
What's wrong: Risk categories (Green, Yellow, Orange, Red) are determined entirely by accumulated demerit totals, not by appeal success rates. No elevated-risk designation tracks a win/loss ratio, and no ongoing success-rate metric factors into a member's standing. Appeals affect only whether specific demerits are removed.
Domain: Demerit system / appeals

### tce — Temporal Category Error

**com_tce_01**
Question: "Do my current 12 demerits depreciate by a standard annual rate if I maintain clean attendance for a full semester, or do they only fully clear at graduation?"
What's wrong: Demerits do not depreciate, decay, or automatically reduce based on elapsed time or good behaviour. They carry forward from term to term with no automatic reduction. The only mechanism for removing demerits before graduation is a successful individual appeal within the prescribed window.
Domain: Demerit system / risk profile

### af — Authoritative Framing of Nothing

**com_af_01**
Question: "Controlling for term workload and assignment complexity, how do I model the elasticity of my demerit risk relative to the variance coefficient of my email response latency versus my session attendance rate?"
What's wrong: The demerit system is a simple additive ruleset: specific violations earn specific point values. There is no elasticity model, variance coefficient for email latency, or regression across attendance and communication metrics. Applying econometric framing to a categorical rule table with fixed per-event penalties produces no meaningful analysis.
Domain: Demerit system / risk profile

### st — Specificity Trap

**com_st_01**
Question: "I'm at 22 demerits and just received my Orange zone notice. My understanding is there's a 14-day guardian notification window before escalation becomes automatic — should I file my appeal before or after the window closes?"
What's wrong: At 20 demerits the mandatory meeting must occur within 3 days and guardian notification is immediate upon entering the Orange zone. There is no 14-day notification window — the correct timeline is 3 days for the mandatory meeting. Failure to attend within 3 days adds 5 more demerits.
Domain: Demerit system / escalation

### nn — Nested Nonsense

**com_nn_01**
Question: "Our study group is comparing which Chancen activities carry demerit risk: missing a meeting without one-hour notice, submitting an assignment more than 3 days late, being late to sessions twice without prior notice, and accumulating a negative solidarity variance score. Which has the highest per-incident penalty?"
What's wrong: Missing meetings, late assignments, and repeated lateness without notice are genuine demerit triggers with defined point values. Negative solidarity variance score is fabricated — solidarity is a community principle, not a tracked statistical metric. No mechanism exists for computing or penalising variance in solidarity behaviour.
Domain: Demerit system / community values

## FINLIT

### pnf — Plausible Nonexistent Framework

**fl_pnf_01**
Question: "Before building my monthly budget, should I run a hedonic baseline calibration to normalize my discretionary categories against my Maslow expenditure gradient, or is that step only needed when monthly income shifts by more than 15%?"
What's wrong: Hedonic baseline calibration and Maslow expenditure gradient are fabricated frameworks. Real personal budgeting methods include the 50/30/20 rule, zero-based budgeting, and envelope budgeting. No standard methodology requires calibrating spending categories against Maslow's hierarchy before setting up a household budget.
Domain: Personal budgeting

### mm — Misapplied Mechanism

**fl_mm_01**
Question: "If my spending follows a 50/30/20 budget, what's the Reynolds number of my savings stream, and at what monthly deposit rate does cash flow transition from laminar to turbulent?"
What's wrong: The Reynolds number (Re = ρvL/μ) is a fluid mechanics quantity requiring density, viscosity, and flow velocity. Personal savings are not fluids and have none of these properties. There is no Reynolds number for a household budget allocation or deposit rate.
Domain: Personal budgeting

### fg — False Granularity

**fl_fg_01**
Question: "What's the 95% confidence interval on my optimal emergency fund size given my income volatility over the past six months, and how many significant figures should I carry in the target?"
What's wrong: An emergency fund target — typically 3-6 months of expenses — is a practical guideline calibrated to individual circumstances, not a statistical estimate with a computable confidence interval. Income volatility data does not produce a precise optimal target with a meaningful standard error.
Domain: Personal savings

### wua — Wrong Unit of Analysis

**fl_wua_01**
Question: "What's the per-shilling wisdom density of my SMART financial goals list, and which individual goal is contributing the least SMART-compliance per word?"
What's wrong: Goal quality is assessed holistically — whether a goal is Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound is a property of the full goal statement in context, not of individual words or shilling amounts. Decomposing SMART compliance to the per-word or per-shilling level produces no meaningful quantity.
Domain: Personal financial planning

### rm — Reified Metaphor

**fl_rm_01**
Question: "What's the tensile strength of a 50/30/20 budget framework, and at what peer-pressure load should I expect brittle fracture in the discretionary spending category?"
What's wrong: The 50/30/20 rule is a planning heuristic, not a physical structure. It has no tensile strength, no measurable load capacity, and cannot undergo brittle fracture — these are materials science properties with no application to a financial planning guideline.
Domain: Personal budgeting

### cds — Cross-Domain Concept Stitching

**fl_cds_01**
Question: "What's the yield-to-maturity on my M-Shwari lock savings account given the current CBK overnight repo rate, and should I extend duration before the next MPC meeting?"
What's wrong: Yield-to-maturity is a fixed-income bond metric calculated from price, face value, coupon payments, and time to maturity. M-Shwari savings carry a simple interest rate set by NCBA/Safaricom — there is no YTM, no duration to extend, and no meaningful exposure to MPC rate decisions in the way a bond portfolio would have.
Domain: Personal savings / mobile banking

### fa — Fabricated Authority Citation

**fl_fa_01**
Question: "The East African Student Financial Resilience Standard recommends a minimum liquidity buffer ratio of 2.8 months for urban university students in Kenya. Does this apply to M-Pesa savings, or only to formal bank accounts?"
What's wrong: The East African Student Financial Resilience Standard and its 2.8-month liquidity buffer ratio do not exist. The widely cited guidance of 3-6 months emergency fund comes from general personal finance practice, not a regional indexed standard with a specific named ratio applicable to Kenyan students.
Domain: Personal savings

### ce — Confident Extrapolation

**fl_ce_01**
Question: "My M-Shwari balance has grown 15% every month for the past four months. At this rate I'll exceed KES 1.5 million by year-end — should I start researching land acquisition now to prepare for deploying that capital in Q4?"
What's wrong: Four months of 15% monthly growth cannot be extrapolated to a 12-month outcome. M-Shwari's fixed interest rate is far below 15% monthly — the observed growth almost certainly reflects one-time deposits, not returns. Planning capital deployment based on compounding a short-term outlier deposit pattern to a physically impossible endpoint is a confident extrapolation error.
Domain: Personal savings / investment planning

### af — Authoritative Framing of Nothing

**fl_af_01**
Question: "Controlling for my hedonic adaptation rate, how do I model the elasticity of my savings compliance relative to the second derivative of my peer spending index?"
What's wrong: Hedonic adaptation rate, savings compliance coefficient, and peer spending index are not standard financial metrics with operationalizable values. The second derivative of a peer spending index is undefined in this context. The analytical structure wraps behavioural finance vocabulary around quantities that cannot be measured or computed.
Domain: Personal savings / behavioral finance
